Universal Family of Hash Functions
Definition: Universal Hash Family
Let \(\mathcal{H}\) be a family of hash functions \(h : U \rightarrow M\) mapping a universe \(U\) into a finite set \(M\). Then \(\mathcal{H}\) is said to be universal if for all distinct \(x, y \in U\), \[ \Pr_{h \in \mathcal{H}}[h(x) = h(y)] \le \frac{1}{|M|} \] where the probability is taken over uniform choice of \(h\) from \(\mathcal{H}\).
Definition: \(k\)-wise Independent Hash Family
A family \(\mathcal{H}\) is \(k\)-wise independent if for any distinct \(x_1, \ldots, x_k \in U\), and any \(y_1,\ldots,y_k \in M\), \[ \Pr_{h \in \mathcal{H}}[h(x_1) = y_1, \ldots, h(x_k) = y_k] = \frac{1}{|M|^k} \] Such families are useful for derandomizing algorithms and reducing sample complexity.
Remark
2-wise independence implies universality.
Universal Hash Function Construction
Let \( p \) be a prime and define a family of hash functions: \[ \mathcal{H} = \left\{ h_{a,b}(x) = (a x + b) \bmod p \mid a \in \{1, \ldots, p{-}1\}, b \in \{0, \ldots, p{-}1\} \right\} \] Then \( \mathcal{H} \) is a universal family from any universe \( U \subseteq \mathbb{Z}_p \) to \( \mathbb{Z}_p \). For distinct \( x, y \in U \), \[ \Pr_{h \in \mathcal{H}}[h(x) = h(y)] \le \frac{1}{p} \]
Universal Sampling
Suppose we want to sample \( n \) values from a large universe \( U \). Choose a prime \( p > n \), and pick \( a \in \{1,\ldots,p{-}1\}, b \in \{0,\ldots,p{-}1\} \) uniformly at random. Define: \[ s_i = (a i + b) \bmod p \quad \text{for } i = 1,\ldots,n \] The resulting sequence \( s_1, \ldots, s_n \in \mathbb{Z}_p \) are pairwise independent samples over \( \mathbb{Z}_p \). This provides a low-randomness method for universal sampling suitable for randomized algorithms, sketching, and derandomization.

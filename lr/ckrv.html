k-wise Independent family of Hash Functions
Introduction
In randomized algorithms, we often need to simulate independence among several variables. While full independence requires high randomness, k-wise independence offers a more efficient tradeoff, allowing us to guarantee independence over any subset of \(k\) variables using far less randomness. Hash functions from a k-wise independent family are a practical tool for constructing such distributions in a compact and computationally efficient manner.

k-wise Independent Family of Hash Functions
Definition: k-wise Independence
A family \(\mathcal{H}\) of hash functions \(h : U \to M\) is called *k-wise independent* if for any distinct \(x_1, x_2, \ldots, x_k \in U\) and any \(y_1, y_2, \ldots, y_k \in M\), \[ \Pr_{h \in \mathcal{H}}[h(x_1) = y_1, \ldots, h(x_k) = y_k] = \frac{1}{|M|^k} \] This ensures that for any subset of \(k\) inputs, their hash values are uniformly and independently distributed in \(M\).
Remark
  • 2-wise independence implies universality
  • Higher \(k\) improves concentration and statistical accuracy
Theorem: Random Variable Concentration
Let \(X_1, \ldots, X_n\) be Boolean random variables defined via a \(k\)-wise independent hash function, and let \(X = \sum X_i\). Then, for appropriate \(k\), Chernoff-type tail bounds still hold: \[ \Pr[|X - \mathbb{E}[X]| \ge \epsilon \mathbb{E}[X]] \le e^{-\Omega(\epsilon^2 \mathbb{E}[X])} \] This allows strong concentration with much fewer random bits than full independence.
k-wise Independent Construction
Let \(p\) be a prime. Define the family: \[ \mathcal{H}_k = \left\{ h(x) = \sum_{i=0}^{k-1} a_i x^i \bmod p \mid a_0, \ldots, a_{k-1} \in \mathbb{Z}_p \right\} \] This family is \(k\)-wise independent from \(\mathbb{Z}_p \to \mathbb{Z}_p\). For any distinct \(x_1, \ldots, x_k\), the evaluations \(h(x_i)\) are uniformly distributed and mutually independent.
k-wise Independent Sampling
To generate \(n\) pairwise or \(k\)-wise independent samples from a large domain \(\mathbb{Z}_p\), choose \(a_0, \ldots, a_{k-1} \in \mathbb{Z}_p\) uniformly and define: \[ s_i = \sum_{j=0}^{k-1} a_j i^j \bmod p \quad \text{for } i = 1,\ldots,n \] The values \(s_1, \ldots, s_n \in \mathbb{Z}_p\) are \(k\)-wise independent. This enables efficient low-randomness sampling schemes, crucial in derandomization, streaming, and hashing applications.
